LITSY

Litsy is the place to share and discover your favorite books with your favorite people. The Translator
IxD | Project at HEAD-Geneva

The Translator is an interactive table, which gives “voice” to inanimate objects.


Technology has already become a certain way of turning magic into reality. What we previously considered impossible today is becoming real via technology. The Translator is yet another “magical item” that tries to turn a magical concept of giving life to an inanimate object into a possible reality. (References: Pygmalion (Greek myth), Toy Story (3D animation), etc.)


This project was a result of a 1-semester Interactive Design workshop with Camille Scherrer at HEAD – Geneva.

Team
UX and Interaction: Mariam Asatryan
Supervision: Camille Scherrer
Tech Support: Pierre Rossel
Video Support: Raphaëlle Mueller
Technical Information
The Translator is a table with a built-in kitchen scale. The scale is connected to an Arduino, which collects the readings from the scale and sends them to a computer. As soon as the scale records a value (certain weight), the computer (through Arduino and Processing programs) chooses a random sound from a sound pool and attaches it to that specific weight. Thus, every time that weight is recorded on the scale, the same sound is played.

Invisible Water: Exhibition in Transat Festival and Musée de la Main
IxD | Project at HEAD-Geneva

Invisible Water was an interactive projection that allowed the visitors to experience the sensation of walking on water.


It was a project created at HEAD – Geneva (Haute École d’Art et de Design – Genève) for the exhibition Touch in Lausanne, Switzerland. The project was displayed during Transat Festival in Lausanne, Switzerland and was on display for 8 months in Musée de la Main – Fondation Claude Verdan (Museum of the Hand) for Touch exhibition (collaboration between Musée de la Main and MUDAC Museum).


The project concentrates on the abstract representation of water and the concept of “touching it” with your feet, since touch does not only incorporate touching with the hands or fingers. The supportive graphics and sounds allow the creation of the ambiance of interaction with water without being in physical contact with it.

Team
UX and Interaction: Mariam Asatryan
Supervision: Camille Scherrer
Tech Support: Matthieu Cherubini and Douglas Edric Stanley
Video Support: Raphaëlle Mueller
Technical Information
Invisible Water consisted of a projector, Microsoft Kinect, and speakers, all powered by a Mac mini, since the projector had to be automatically powered on and off during the days of the exhibition. The Kinect was adjusted to the projected area, allowing the detection of objects in that area and their coordinates. Once the objects’ locations were detected, the projector displayed generating circles, creating the illusion of walking on water. The projected water (circles) also pushed the geometrical leaves, thus, allowing the participants to be able to push away the falling leaves by creating water circles. In addition, the project incorporated a randomly generated rain, compilation of many falling leaves and circles accompanied with sounds. The project was created in Java with Processing and Kinect libraries.
Non-Friction World: Inverse Everything Exhibition
IxD | Project at HEAD-Geneva

Non-Friction World is one of the four projects of the Inverse Everything exhibition, presented during Salone Internazionale del Mobile furniture fair in Milan, Italy by HEAD-Geneva. The four inversed scenarios were world with multi-gravity, world without friction, telepathic world, and world with living appliances.


In the "World Without Friction" project the aim was to envision how the world could develop in the setting of non-friction, where everything is in continuous movement. We envisioned floating cities, since the life developed in non-friction is adapted to being unattached to the world, thus, creating a random life style, lacking any kind of attachment (physical and, in the case of inhabitants, emotional as well). The goal of the project was to critically reflect on the tendency of Earth inhabitants to organization and control over life, as well as to experiment with the interesting outcomes of such an imaginary scenario.

Team
Supervision: Auger-Loizeau and El Ultimo Grito
Team: Mariam Asatryan, Maisa Moraes, Khalil Klouche, Michael Mouyal, and Faustine Lavorel
Technical Information
The interactive table was created using strong fans to replicate an air hockey table to allow the geometrical forms float. The videos and the connection schedule were created using Adobe AfterEffects, Soundbooth, Audition, 3D software and Processing.
